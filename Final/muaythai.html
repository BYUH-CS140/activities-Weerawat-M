Muay Thai, also known as Thai boxing or the "Art of Eight Limbs," is a traditional Thai martial art and full-contact sport that uses fists, elbows, knees, and shins for striking, along with clinching and sweeps. It gained global popularity in the late 20th century as Thai fighters competed internationally. The sport is regulated by the Professional Boxing Association of Thailand and the Sports Authority of Thailand. Muay Thai is related to other Southeast Asian martial arts like Muay Boran, Lethwei, and Tomoi. Practitioners are called Nak Muay, with foreigners known as Nak Muay Farang.
